## 1.1 DISSOLVED OXYGEN (DO) — COMPLETE GUIDE

### DO Daily Pattern in AP Shrimp Ponds
| Time | DO Status | Risk Level | Action Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| 12 AM – 3 AM | Declining | MODERATE | Aerators must run |
| 3 AM – 6 AM | LOWEST POINT | CRITICAL | ALL aerators running; farmer must check |
| 6 AM – 8 AM | Rising slowly | MODERATE–HIGH | Check shrimp behavior at banks |
| 8 AM – 4 PM | Rising (photosynthesis) | LOW | Normal operations |
| 4 PM – 6 PM | Peak DO | VERY LOW | Can reduce aeration slightly |
| 6 PM – 12 AM | Declining | LOW–MODERATE | Aerators ON |

### DO Crash — Causes in AP
| Cause | When It Happens in AP | Warning Sign |
|---|---|---|
| Dense algae bloom crash | Overnight after peak bloom day | Water turns gray/white suddenly |
| Overfeeding | 4–6 hours after feed | High ammonia smell; feed residue |
| Hot cloudless day → hot night | April–June in AP | No wind; still water |
| Heavy rain | Monsoon season; rain event | Water surface cooling + stratification |
| Algae toxin bloom (cyanobacteria) | Post-rain; fertilizer overuse | Blue-green water color |
| Black soil / H₂S release | Bottom disturbance | Rotten egg smell from water |
| Overcrowding | Any time stocking >60 PL/m² | Shrimp at banks; reduced feeding |
| Paddlewheel / aerator breakdown | Any time | Immediate check all equipment |

### DO Emergency Protocol — Shrimp
**IF SHRIMP ARE SURFACING OR GATHERING AT BANKS:**
1. ⚡ Run ALL paddlewheels and aerators at 100% — RIGHT NOW
2. 🚫 STOP FEEDING immediately — decomposing feed uses DO
3. 💧 Open water exchange inlet (clean water only)
4. ➕ Add H₂O₂ (Hydrogen Peroxide 35%): 500 mL per acre in critical zones
5. 🐛 Remove all dead shrimp immediately — rotting consumes DO
6. ❌ DO NOT add lime, probiotics, or any chemicals during crash
7. After stabilization: Find and fix the root cause

### DO and Feeding — AP Rule of Thumb
| DO Reading | Feeding Action |
|---|---|
| >5 mg/L | Normal feeding |
| 4–5 mg/L | Reduce feed 20% |
| 3–4 mg/L | Reduce feed 50%; increase aeration |
| <3 mg/L | STOP FEEDING; emergency aeration |
| <2 mg/L | EMERGENCY — all aerators; water exchange |

## 1.2 pH — COMPLETE MANAGEMENT GUIDE

### pH Daily Pattern (AP Shrimp Ponds)
| Time | pH Range | Reason | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early morning (5–7 AM) | 7.5–7.8 | CO₂ accumulation overnight | Normal — observe |
| Morning (8–10 AM) | 7.8–8.2 | Photosynthesis begins | Optimal range |
| Afternoon (2–4 PM) | 8.2–8.8 | Peak photosynthesis | Normal; watch >9.0 |
| Evening (6–8 PM) | 8.0–8.5 | Photosynthesis slowing | Normal |

**Normal pH daily swing:** 0.5–1.0 units — ACCEPTABLE
**Concerning pH swing:** >1.5 units — Unstable bloom; take action
**Dangerous pH swing:** >2.0 units — Risk of crash; emergency action

### pH Problems and Solutions
| Problem | Reading | Cause | Immediate Action | Product |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Low pH (acidic) | <7.5 | Heavy rain; peat soil; low alkalinity | Apply lime immediately | CaCO₃: 50–100 kg/acre |
| pH crash (severe) | <7.0 | Heavy monsoon rain; bloom collapse | Emergency lime application | CaCO₃: 100–200 kg/acre |
| High pH (alkaline) | >8.8 daytime | Dense algae bloom | 20% water exchange; reduce fertilizer | — |
| Very high pH | >9.2 | Extreme bloom | Emergency 30% water exchange | — |
| Unstable swing | >1.5 units/day | Inconsistent algae bloom | Stabilize bloom with probiotics; lime | Dolomite: 50 kg/acre |

### pH and Ammonia — Critical AP Summer Note
At pH 8.5 and temperature 30°C (very common AP April–June):
- 15–20% of Total Ammonia Nitrogen (TAN) becomes toxic un-ionized NH₃
- This means: if TAN = 1.0 mg/L, then ~0.15–0.20 mg/L is TOXIC
- **AP summer ponds with poor aeration are extremely vulnerable**
- Action: Test pH AND ammonia together; treat both

### Low Salinity — Special Management (AP Inland Ponds)
**Problem:** At salinity <10 ppt, shrimp cannot get enough Ca, Mg, K from water
- Molting becomes difficult → soft shell → cannibalism
- Growth slows
- Immune system weakened

## 1.5 AMMONIA — PRACTICAL SHRIMP GUIDE

### Sources of Ammonia in AP Shrimp Ponds
1. **Shrimp excretion** — main natural source (60–70% of total)
2. **Leftover feed decomposition** — overfeeding is #1 controllable source
3. **Dead shrimp** — 1 dead shrimp produces significant ammonia
4. **Algae die-off** — overnight bloom crash
5. **Organic pond bottom** — accumulated sludge
6. **Post-rain flush** — washes stored organic matter into water column

### Ammonia Control — Step-by-Step
| Step | Action | Product | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Reduce feeding 50% immediately | — | Always first step |
| 2 | Maximum aeration | — | DO helps nitrification |
| 3 | Apply zeolite | 50–100 kg/acre | Binds ammonia fast (12–24h) |
| 4 | Water exchange 20–30% | Fresh/clean water only | Dilutes TAN |
| 5 | Apply probiotics (next day) | Growel Pond Pro: 2 kg/acre | Bacillus break down organics |
| 6 | Retest ammonia after 24h | — | Check if below 1 mg/L |
| 7 | Find root cause | Feed tray check; DO history | Prevent recurrence |

- Alkalinity (CaCO₃ equivalent) is the pH BUFFER of the pond

## 1.8 HYDROGEN SULFIDE (H₂S) — SILENT KILLER

### H₂S in AP Shrimp Ponds
- Produced in anaerobic (no oxygen) zones of pond bottom
- Black soil at bottom = H₂S present
- Very toxic even at 0.002 mg/L — far more toxic than ammonia

### Signs of H₂S Problem
- Black mud color at pond bottom
- Rotten egg smell when pond is disturbed
- Black patches on water surface in still morning conditions
- Sudden unexplained mortality at pond bottom areas
- Shrimp with black gills (chronic exposure)

### H₂S Emergency Response
1. Do NOT disturb pond bottom — do not till or stir
2. Run ALL bottom aerators (diffusers) immediately
3. Apply agricultural lime to pond margins: 50–100 kg/acre
4. 20–30% water exchange from surface
5. Apply zeolite to bottom: 100 kg/acre
6. STOP FEEDING until H₂S controlled

### H₂S Prevention (AP Conditions)
| Prevention Method | Action | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Bottom aeration | Run diffusers 24/7 | Continuous |
| Avoid overfeeding | Feed tray management | Every feeding |
| Probiotics | Growel Pond Pro: 2 kg/acre | Every 7–10 days |
| Reduce organic load | Siphon pond bottom | Every 7–14 days in intensive ponds |
| Lime application | 20–50 kg/acre | Fortnightly |

### Vannamei ABW Sampling Protocol (Every 7 Days)
1. Use cast net or hand net (early morning — shrimp active)
2. Collect minimum 50 shrimp from 3–4 locations per pond
3. Weigh all 50 shrimp together → divide by 50 = ABW
4. Also record: shell condition (hard/soft), gut color, hepatopancreas color
5. Calculate survival estimate from weekly trends
6. Adjust feed ration: Biomass (kg) × Feeding Rate (%) = Daily Feed (kg)

### Feed Calculation Formula (Vannamei)
```
Estimated Biomass (kg) = Stocked PLs × Estimated Survival % × ABW (g) / 1000
Daily Feed (kg) = Estimated Biomass × Feeding Rate %
Feed per meal = Daily Feed ÷ Number of meals
Feed tray amount = 0.2–0.3% of daily feed per tray
```

Tiger shrimp is NOCTURNAL — feeds primarily at night. Adjust feeding accordingly:

| Time | Meal % | Notes |
|---|---|---|

| 06:00 PM | 30% | Major meal — dusk is active feeding time |
| 10:00 PM | 40% | LARGEST meal — nocturnal peak activity |

**Feed tray check:** At 9 PM and 11 PM (after night feeds)
**Tiger shrimp needs:** Attractants in feed (squid meal, fish meal) for good palatability

### Tiger Shrimp — AP Farming Challenges
1. **WSSV vulnerability:** Tiger shrimp is MORE susceptible to WSSV than vannamei
   - Cold temperature (<24°C) dramatically increases WSSV risk
   - AP winter (December–February) is peak WSSV risk for monodon
   - Must maintain temperature >25°C; avoid cold-snap exposure

2. **Higher salinity requirement:** Unlike vannamei, tiger shrimp needs 15+ ppt
   - Inland AP ponds with <10 ppt salinity are NOT suitable for tiger
   - Coastal ponds (Nellore, East Godavari, Srikakulam) are preferred

3. **Higher feed protein requirement:** 36–42% protein vs 32–35% for vannamei
   - Feeds: Growel Tigeron, Waterbase Tiger Bay XL, Skretting Kuroline
   - Do not use vannamei feed for tiger — suboptimal nutrition

4. **Yellow Head Disease (YHD):** Near 100% mortality in 2–4 days — no treatment
   - AP coastal tiger shrimp farms must maintain strict biosecurity

5. **Slow return on investment:** 150–180 day grow-out vs 90–120 for vannamei
   - BUT: premium price (₹500–900+/kg vs ₹300–500 for vannamei)

### Freshwater Prawn Male Types (Dominance System)
This is the MOST UNIQUE aspect of M. rosenbergii farming:

| Male Type | Local Name | Description | Behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| Blue Claw (BC) | Large claw males | Large; blue-colored large chelae (claws) | DOMINANT; suppresses others |

- Solution: **Periodic selective harvest of Blue Claw males** (every 30–45 days)
- This releases dominance suppression → other males grow faster
- Most farms use this "partial harvesting" or "topping" approach

### Freshwater Prawn — Special Management
1. **Hiding structures:** Prawn are territorial and cannibalistic — provide shelter

   - Reduces aggression and cannibalism significantly

2. **Gender separation:** Monosex male culture gives better uniform growth
   - Female market: smaller size; lower value; sell separately

3. **Jumping:** Like murrel; cover ponds/nursery tanks with nets

4. **Soft water:** In very soft water (<50 mg/L hardness), add dolomite for minerals

5. **Feed placement:** Prawn are bottom feeders — sinking feed preferred

## 3.1 WHITE SPOT SYNDROME VIRUS (WSSV)
## Telugu: వైట్ స్పాట్ వ్యాధి

**Pathogen:** White Spot Syndrome Virus (dsDNA; Nimaviridae)
**Mortality:** 80–100% within 3–7 days
**Species:** ALL penaeid shrimp — vannamei, tiger, all penaeids
**No treatment — prevention is everything**
**AP Risk:** Year-round; peaks in November–February (cold months) and after sudden temp drop

### Symptoms — Complete List
| Symptom | Detail | How to Check |
|---|---|---|
| White spots on shell | 0.5–2mm; calcified deposits under cuticle | Press carapace — spots DON'T rub off (unlike external dirt) |
| Reddish-pink color | Body, appendages, tail turn reddish-pink | Visual; most obvious in affected ponds |
| Gathering at banks | Shrimp swim to pond edges, banks, aerators | Visual dawn observation |
| Surface swimming | Listless; lethargic; erratic | Visual |
| Reduced/no feeding | Feed tray has residue | Feed tray check |
| Mass mortality | Sudden; peaks day 3–7 | Daily mortality count |
| Soft shell | Despite no recent molting | Touch test |
| Empty gut | No food visible in translucent gut | Visual inspection of live shrimp |

### WSSV Triggers in AP (AVOID THESE)
| Trigger | When in AP | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Temperature drop <24°C | November–February nights | Monitor; avoid stocking in November–December |
| Sudden temperature change >3°C/24h | Cold front; rain event | Pre-rain management; maintain aeration |
| Stress from handling/sampling | Any time | Minimize handling; only sample early morning |
| Wild shrimp/crab entering pond | Always | Tight perimeter fencing; mesh screens on inlet |
| Bird entry (herons, kingfishers) | Always — AP coastal zones have many | Bird exclusion nets — mandatory |
| Contaminated water intake | Always | Chlorinate: 20–30 ppm; 24h contact |
| Low DO <4 mg/L | Summer nights; overcast days | 24/7 aeration |
| Infected seed (PLs) | Stocking time | PCR test before stocking — NON-NEGOTIABLE |

### WSSV Diagnosis
| Method | Procedure | Reliability |
|---|---|---|
| Visual inspection | White spots on carapace that don't rub off | Presumptive (75–85% accuracy) |
| PCR test | Send to AQIM lab / CIBA / RGCA / Aqualab | GOLD STANDARD — 100% |
| Wet mount microscopy | Scraping of gills/cuticle; look for infected cells | Moderate |
| Post-mortem gross | Whitish hepatopancreas; necrotic gills | Supports diagnosis |

### WSSV Management Decision Tree
```
WSSV SUSPECTED OR CONFIRMED →

1. STOP FEEDING immediately
2. Check PCR (if available)
3. Assess pond status:

   Pond age <30 days (small shrimp <3g)?
   → Economic loss; difficult to harvest; consider:
      - Emergency harvest with very small mesh net
      - OR continue with supportive care + strict biosecurity
      - Mortality will likely be 80–100% regardless

   Pond age 30–60 days (shrimp 3–12g)?
   → Partial economic value; attempt emergency harvest
   → Sell at discount to local market if possible
   → Biosecurity lockdown of pond

   Pond age >60 days (shrimp >12g)?
   → EMERGENCY HARVEST IMMEDIATELY
   → Contact processing plant / local buyer for distress purchase
   → Even at discount — better than total loss
   → Every day of delay = more mortality = more loss

4. AFTER HARVEST OR MORTALITY:
   - Do NOT release pond water to canal/sea without chlorination
   - Chlorinate all discharge: 30 ppm active chlorine × 24h contact
   - Drain completely; dry 3–4 weeks minimum
   - Till/plow pond bottom
   - Apply quicklime (CaO): 500–1000 kg/acre (dry)
   - Wait 4 weeks before refilling
   - PCR test water before next stocking
```

### WSSV Supportive Care (Buys Time — Does Not Cure)
| Product | Dose | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Vitamin C | 500g/acre water + 3 g/kg feed | Immune system support |
| Beta-glucan supplement | 2 g/kg feed | Non-specific immune activation |
| Alltech Yea-Sacc | 2 kg/MT feed | Immune modulation |
| Probiotics (Growel Pond Pro) | 2 kg/acre | Reduce secondary infection |

## 3.2 EARLY MORTALITY SYNDROME / AHPND
## (Acute Hepatopancreatic Necrosis Disease)
## Telugu: EMS వ్యాధి | Hepatopancreas వ్యాధి

**Pathogen:** Vibrio parahaemolyticus carrying pirAB toxin genes (VpAHPND)
**Mortality:** 70–100% within 20–30 days of stocking
**Species:** Vannamei primarily; tiger shrimp also
**Peak risk period:** FIRST 30 DAYS after stocking
**AP Risk:** Very high in AP — organic-loaded ponds, hot weather accelerates Vibrio

### EMS Symptoms — In Order of Appearance
| Day Post-Infection | Symptom | Check Method |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1–2 | Reduced feeding (first sign) | Feed tray — more residue than usual |
| Day 2–3 | Shrimp near aerators; lethargic | Visual dawn observation |
| Day 3–5 | Hepatopancreas (HP) pale/shrunken | Pick up shrimp; look through shell at HP area (behind head) |
| Day 3–5 | Empty gut visible | Shrimp looks "empty" internally |
| Day 4–7 | Soft shell despite not molting | Touch test |
| Day 5–10 | Mass mortality | Count dead shrimp |

### How to Check Hepatopancreas (HP) — AP Farmer Guide
1. Pick up 5–10 live/moribund shrimp
2. Look at the head region (cephalothorax) — HP is the orangish/brownish organ visible through shell
3. **HEALTHY HP:** Full, plump, orange-brown color; fills the head region
4. **EMS HP:** Pale yellow to white; shrunken; doesn't fill head region; atrophied
5. **Cut the shrimp head open** with scissors to see HP directly
6. Pale/shrunken HP in first 30 days = HIGH PROBABILITY EMS/AHPND

### EMS — Emergency Response Protocol
```
EMS SUSPECTED (first 30 days; pale HP; mass mortality)?

IMMEDIATE (within 1 hour):
□ STOP FEEDING completely
□ Run ALL aerators at maximum
□ Call veterinarian / aquaculture officer
□ Collect 10–15 live/moribund shrimp in wet ice for PCR

WITHIN 2 HOURS:
□ 20–30% water exchange with clean water
□ Apply Growel Pond Pro or Biostadt Optibact: 2 kg/acre
   (Bacillus competition against Vibrio)
□ Apply Vitamin C: 500g/acre in water
□ Remove all dead shrimp from pond

WITHIN 24 HOURS (after vet consultation):
□ If bacterial confirmed → Florfenicol: 10–15 mg/kg BW in feed
   (Vet prescription required)
□ Apply Intas Hepatocare Aqua: 10 g/kg in feed
□ Resume feeding at 30% of normal rate
□ Continue probiotics every 3 days

DECISION:
□ Pond age >45 days + >7g ABW → Emergency partial harvest
□ Pond age <30 days + mortality >30% in 24h → Evaluate total loss
```

### EMS vs WSSV — How to Differentiate

| Feature | EMS/AHPND | WSSV |
|---|---|---|
| Timing | First 0–30 days | Any time; peaks after cold/stress |
| HP appearance | Pale/shrunken/atrophied | Usually normal or slightly pale |
| White spots on shell | ABSENT | PRESENT (key difference) |
| Body color | Normal or pale | Reddish-pink |
| Speed of mortality | 5–10 days | 3–7 days |
| Shell condition | May be soft | Can have spots |
| Lab confirmation | PCR VpAHPND | PCR WSSV |

## 3.3 VIBRIOSIS — LUMINESCENT AND NON-LUMINESCENT

**Species:** All shrimp; all life stages (larvae to grow-out)
**AP Risk:** VERY HIGH in summer (April–June) — Vibrio explodes above 30°C

### Vibriosis Forms in AP

**Form 1 — Luminescent Vibriosis (Larvae/PL Stage)**
| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Affected stage | PL1–PL30 (hatchery + first pond month) |
| Key sign | Pond/tank GLOWS BLUE-GREEN at night — bioluminescence |
| Mortality | Very high — can kill 70–100% of PLs |
| Cause | V. harveyi (produces luciferase — bioluminescent enzyme) |

**Form 2 — Black Spot / Shell Disease (Grow-Out)**
| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Affected stage | Any grow-out size |
| Signs | Black/brown lesions on shell; tail rot; rostrum erosion; hemorrhagic spots in muscle |
| Mortality | Moderate — rarely causes mass mortality alone; weakens shrimp for other diseases |

| Condition | Vibrio Risk Level |
|---|---|
| Temperature >30°C + DO <4 mg/L | EXTREME |
| Temperature >28°C + high organic load | HIGH |
| Temperature 25–28°C + good water quality | MODERATE |
| Temperature <25°C | LOW |
| After heavy rain + organic flush | HIGH |
| TCBS agar shows green colonies | CONFIRMED Vibrio |

### Vibrio Control Protocol
| Step | Action | Product | Dose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Reduce feeding 30–50% | — | — |
| 2 | Increase aeration | — | 24/7 |
| 3 | Apply probiotics (Bacillus) | Growel Pond Pro | 2 kg/acre |
| 3 | OR: Apply probiotics (Bacillus) | Biostadt Optibact | 500g/acre |
| 4 | Water exchange | 20–30% | Clean source only |
| 5 | Apply BKC if severe bloom | Aqua BKC | 100–150 mL/acre (diluted) |
| 6 | Wait 48h then re-apply probiotics | — | — |
| 7 | Antibiotics if systemic (vet required) | Florfenicol / Oxytetracycline | Per vet dose |

### TCBS Agar — Field Diagnostic for Vibrio
- Sample: 1 mL pond water or hemolymph from shrimp
- Streak on TCBS agar (Yellow Sulfite Agar)
- Incubate 24h at 35°C
- **Yellow colonies = V. cholerae / V. alginolyticus**
- **Green colonies = V. parahaemolyticus / V. harveyi (most dangerous)**
- Total Vibrio count >10³ CFU/mL in water = HIGH RISK; treat immediately

## 3.4 EHP — ENTEROCYTOZOON HEPATOPENAEI (Microsporidiosis)
## Telugu: EHP వ్యాధి | మందగమనం వ్యాధి (Slow growth disease)

**Pathogen:** Enterocytozoon hepatopenaei (microsporidian parasite)
**Species:** Vannamei (primary); Tiger shrimp
**Mortality:** LOW direct mortality — but causes SEVERE economic loss from poor growth
**AP Impact:** Increasingly prevalent in AP farms; often misdiagnosed

### EHP — Why It's an Economic Disaster
- Shrimp survive but DON'T GROW normally
- FCR doubles or triples — you keep feeding but shrimp barely gain weight
- High variation in size (CV >20%) — sorting needed repeatedly
- Same pond: some shrimp 5g, some shrimp 15g at same DOC
- By harvest: ABW far below target → low income

### EHP Symptoms
| Symptom | How to Identify |
|---|---|
| Very slow growth | ABW at DOC 60 is 3–5g instead of expected 6–10g |
| HIGH size variation | CV% >20% (many sizes in same pond) |
| White/chalky muscles | Severe: tail muscle appears white/opaque |
| Pale hepatopancreas | HP lighter than normal; reduced enzyme activity |
| Shrimp thin-looking | Despite eating; poor nutrient absorption |
| White fecal strings | Sometimes — white feces floating (associated but not exclusive) |
| Poor FCR | 2.5–4.0 despite good water quality |

### EHP Diagnosis
| Method | Details |
|---|---|
| PCR test (gold standard) | HP sample to AQIM / CIBA / Aqualab — 100% accurate |
| Histopathology | HP tubule cells show EHP spores under microscope |
| Fecal squash | Spores visible in fresh feces under microscope (skill required) |
| Field indicator | High size variation + poor growth + no other obvious disease = suspect EHP |

### EHP Management (No Cure Available)
```
IF EHP CONFIRMED →

IMMEDIATE FARM MANAGEMENT:
□ Assess economics: if ABW <40% of target at mid-culture → plan early harvest
□ Increase Vitamin C in feed: 3 g/kg (reduce parasite impact)
□ Apply Kemin Clostat Aqua: 500g/MT feed (gut health support)
□ Apply Growel Gut Pro: 10 g/kg feed (probiotic gut support)
□ Reduce stocking density in next crop

NEXT CROP PREVENTION:
□ Thorough pond DRYING: minimum 4 weeks until soil cracks fully
□ LIME with quicklime (CaO): 1000 kg/acre — kills EHP spores
□ Till/plow pond bottom: 30 cm deep — expose spores to UV
□ PCR-test PLs from hatchery BEFORE stocking
□ Source PLs from Avanti or CP hatcheries (SPF certified)
□ Do not source PLs from hatcheries supplying to EHP-positive farms
```

## 3.9 SOFT SHELL SYNDROME
## Telugu: మెత్తని చిప్ప వ్యాధి

**Cause:** Mineral deficiency (Ca, Mg, K); molting cycle disruption; disease stress; low salinity
**Species:** All shrimp; most common in vannamei in low-salinity AP ponds

### Soft Shell Symptoms
- Shell remains soft >24 hours after molting (normal hardening: 6–12 hours)
- Shrimp appear limp/flaccid
- Cannibalism increases dramatically during soft shell period
- High mortality due to being eaten by tank-mates

### Soft Shell Treatment
| Product | Dose | Frequency | Expected Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growel Aqua Mineral (chelated) | 5 kg/acre (water) + 10 g/kg feed | After every molting event | Shell hardening in 24–48h |
| Intas AquaMins | 5 kg/acre water | After rain; after molting | Mineral replenishment |
| Dolomite | 50–100 kg/acre | Fortnightly | Ca + Mg baseline |
| Potassium chloride (KCl) | 5–10 kg/acre | Low-salinity ponds | K+ for ion regulation |

**AP Context:** Critical in West Godavari and Krishna low-salinity inland ponds — apply mineral supplement religiously after every rain event and after molting events.

## 3.10 SHELL DISEASE (Bacterial Shell Disease — BSD)
**Pathogen:** Vibrio, Aeromonas, Pseudomonas spp.

### Symptoms
- Brown/black pitted lesions on carapace and appendages
- Erosion of rostrum, antennae, uropods
- Lesions progressively deepen if untreated
- Soft/fragile shell

### Treatment
- Improve water quality; 20% water exchange
- Povidone iodine bath: 50 ppm × 15 min
- Potassium permanganate bath: 10 ppm × 30 min
- Probiotics application to water
- Mineral supplementation

## 3.11 GREGARINES (Protozoan Gut Parasites)

**Pathogen:** Nematopsis spp., Cephalolobus spp.
**Species:** All shrimp
**Mortality:** Low — but causes gut damage, poor FCR, white gut appearance

### Symptoms
- White/translucent elongated bodies visible in gut (through shell)
- Pale white gut appearance
- Reduced feeding
- Poor growth / poor FCR without other obvious disease

### Treatment
- No effective treatment
- Probiotics help restore gut microbiome
- Garlic extract: 10–20 g/kg feed — some anti-gregarine effect
- Good water quality and feed management
- Will reduce naturally with good pond management

---

## 3.12 GILL FOULING — VORTICELLA / ZOOTHAMNIUM (Epibionts)

**Type:** Protozoan epibionts — attach to gills and shell surface
**Species:** All shrimp; especially in ponds with high organic load
**Signs:** Fuzzy/hairy appearance on gills; gills look "dirty" under microscope

### Treatment
- Increase water exchange to reduce organic load
- Formalin bath: 25–50 ppm × 1 hour (carefully — aerate)
- Freshwater dip for saltwater shrimp: 30 min (osmotic shock kills epibionts)
- Reduce organic load in pond

## BIOSECURITY — AP SHRIMP POND STANDARDS

### Farm Entry Biosecurity
| Risk Area | Control Measure | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Farm gate | Disinfection foot bath: 20 ppm chlorine | Prevent pathogen tracking |
| Vehicle entry | Wheel disinfection spray or mat | Vehicle tires carry soil and disease |
| Workers | Change clothes before entering farm | Human-carried pathogens |
| Pond entry | Foot dip per pond: 20 ppm chlorine | Cross-contamination between ponds |
| Equipment | One set per pond; disinfect before sharing | Equipment spreads disease rapidly |
| Visitors | Limit; provide farm clothing; disinfect | Visitor shoes = pathogen risk |

### Wildlife Biosecurity — AP Specific
| Wildlife | Control | AP Context |
|---|---|---|
| Herons / egrets | Bird exclusion nets over ponds | Very common in AP coastal zones; WSSV vectors |
| Kingfishers | Bird exclusion nets | Small but effective WSSV carriers |
| Wild crabs | Perimeter bund fencing; inspection | Major WSSV carrier entering from mangroves |
| Wild shrimp | Fine mesh intake screens; chlorination | Can carry WSSV into pond |
| Water rats | Perimeter fencing; trapping | Can carry diseases; damage bunds |

### Disease Outbreak Containment — AP Protocol
1. Isolate affected pond — NO water sharing; NO equipment sharing
2. Dedicate one worker to affected pond — does NOT go to other ponds
3. Foot bath at exit: 50 ppm chlorine
4. Dead animals: collect in buckets; bury 1m deep with 1:1 lime ratio
5. NO water release from affected pond to canal/waterway without chlorination (30 ppm × 24h)
6. Notify AP Aquaculture Officer (mandatory for WSSV, EHP, AHPND outbreaks)
7. Document everything: date, mortality count, water parameters, actions taken

## 🚨 SHRIMP AT SURFACE / BANKS (DAWN):
1. CHECK DO meter — if <3 mg/L: OXYGEN EMERGENCY
2. Run ALL aerators NOW
3. STOP FEEDING
4. Call Aqua Doctor / aquaculture officer

## 🚨 MASS MORTALITY STARTED:
1. STOP FEEDING
2. Run aerators
3. Do NOT drain pond to canal
4. Collect 10–15 moribund shrimp in wet ice — send for PCR
5. Check: DO → pH → temperature → water color → HP color

## 🚨 WHITE SPOTS ON SHELL:
1. Confirm spots don't rub off — WSSV suspected
2. STOP FEEDING
3. Assess pond age → if >60 days + >10g ABW: EMERGENCY HARVEST
4. Chlorinate any water discharge: 30 ppm × 24h
5. Call vet / aquaculture officer

## 🚨 PALE SHRUNKEN HP (first 30 days):
1. EMS/AHPND suspected
2. STOP FEEDING
3. Maximum aeration + 30% water exchange
4. Apply Growel Pond Pro: 2 kg/acre
5. Vitamin C: 500g/acre in water
6. Call vet → antibiotic (Florfenicol) if confirmed bacterial

## 🚨 pH CRASH AFTER RAIN:
1. Apply lime: 50–100 kg/acre IMMEDIATELY
2. Stop or reduce feed 50%
3. Check and record pH every 2 hours
4. Resume feeding when pH >7.5

## 🚨 HIGH AMMONIA (TAN >2 mg/L):
1. Stop feeding or reduce 50%
2. Apply zeolite: 100 kg/acre
3. Water exchange: 20–30%
4. Increase aeration
5. Apply probiotics NEXT DAY (48h after any BKC)
