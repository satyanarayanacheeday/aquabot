# Aquaculture Knowledge Base

## Water Quality Parameters

### Dissolved Oxygen (DO)
- Ideal range: 4-8 mg/L, best above 5 mg/L
- Below 3 mg/L is critical — shrimp will surface and may die
- Lowest at dawn (4-6 AM), highest in afternoon
- Improve with: aerators, paddle wheels, reduce stocking density
- High feed rates consume more oxygen through decomposition
- Algae produce oxygen during day but consume at night

### pH
- Ideal range: 7.5-8.5
- Below 7.0: acidic, causes stress, poor growth, soft shell
- Above 9.0: ammonia becomes toxic, gill damage
- Morning pH is lowest, afternoon highest
- Lime (CaCO3) raises pH, organic acids lower it
- Sudden pH changes (>0.5 in 24hr) cause stress

### Temperature
- Ideal for Vannamei: 28-32°C
- Below 25°C: reduced feeding, slow growth, susceptible to disease
- Above 34°C: stress, low DO, increased ammonia toxicity
- Reduce feeding by 10-20% during temperature extremes

### Ammonia (NH3)
- Total ammonia nitrogen (TAN) should be below 0.1 mg/L
- Toxic form (un-ionized NH3) increases with higher pH and temperature
- Sources: decomposing feed, excretion, dead organisms
- Control: reduce feed, increase aeration, probiotics, water exchange

### Salinity
- Vannamei optimal: 15-25 ppt
- Can tolerate 1-40 ppt but growth affected outside optimal
- Sudden changes (>5 ppt per day) cause osmotic stress

### Alkalinity
- Ideal: 120-150 mg/L
- Below 80: pH unstable, plankton crashes
- Maintain with dolomite or sodium bicarbonate

## Common Shrimp Diseases

### White Spot Syndrome Virus (WSSV)
- Signs: white spots on shell, rapid death, lethargy, reduced feeding
- Caused by: WSSV virus, enters through contaminated water/carriers
- No treatment available
- Prevention: biosecurity, PCR testing PL, clean water, avoid wild crabs/carriers
- Action: isolate affected ponds, emergency harvest if possible

### Early Mortality Syndrome / AHPND
- Signs: pale/atrophied hepatopancreas, empty gut, mass mortality in first 30 days
- Caused by: Vibrio parahaemolyticus carrying toxin genes
- Prevention: probiotics, clean pond bottom, maintain good water quality
- Action: reduce feed, apply probiotics, consult expert

### Vibriosis
- Signs: luminescent (glowing) larvae, black spots, shell erosion
- Caused by: Vibrio species bacteria
- Prevention: maintain water quality, use probiotics, proper pond prep
- Green vibrio colonies on TCBS agar indicate infection

### White Feces Disease (WFD)
- Signs: white fecal strings floating, reduced feeding, slow growth
- Associated with: EHP (Enterocytozoon hepatopenaei) microsporidian
- Prevention: treat pond bottom, use probiotics, maintain DO
- Action: reduce feeding 30-50%, apply garlic/herb extracts, probiotics

### Loose Shell Syndrome
- Signs: shell loosely attached, soft body, meat ratio drops
- Caused by: mineral deficiency, rapid growth, poor water quality
- Action: add minerals (calcium, magnesium), check alkalinity

## Feed Management

### Feed Rate Guidelines (Vannamei)
- DOC 1-30: 10-8% body weight
- DOC 31-60: 6-4% body weight
- DOC 61-90: 3.5-2.5% body weight
- DOC 91+: 2.5-1.8% body weight

### Feed Check Tray
- Use check trays to monitor actual consumption
- If feed remains after 2 hours, reduce by 10%
- If consumed in < 1 hour, can increase by 5-10%
- Check tray amount: 1-2% of total feed

### FCR (Feed Conversion Ratio)
- Good FCR for Vannamei: 1.2-1.5
- Above 1.8 indicates inefficiency
- Factors: feed quality, water quality, stocking density, health

## Pond Management

### Pond Preparation
1. Drain completely, dry for 7-14 days
2. Remove black soil, crack bottom
3. Apply lime: 200-500 kg/hectare
4. Fill water through filter (300 micron)
5. Fertilize for plankton bloom
6. Wait for good water color (brown/green) before stocking

### Stocking
- Recommended density: 30-60 PL/sqm for intensive
- PL quality: PCR tested, active, uniform size
- Acclimatize for 30-60 minutes (temperature, salinity match)
- Best stocking time: early morning or evening

### Aeration
- Minimum 1 HP per 400 kg expected harvest
- Run aerators at night full time
- Morning hours (3-6 AM) most critical
- Emergency: run all aerators if shrimp surfacing

## Harvest Guidelines
- Vannamei market size: 20-30g (count 30-50)
- Best price seasons vary by region
- Partial harvest can manage density
- Stop feeding 6-12 hours before harvest
- Harvest in cool hours (early morning)
