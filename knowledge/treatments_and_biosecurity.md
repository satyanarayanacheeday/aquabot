|---|---|---|---|---|
| Florfenicol | Aeromonas, Edwardsiella, Streptococcus, Vibrio | 10–15 mg/kg body weight | 7–14 days | 15–30 days |

| Amoxicillin | Streptococcus, Aeromonas | 80 mg/kg body weight | 10 days | 14 days |

| Enrofloxacin | Aeromonas, Vibrio (resistance common) | 5–10 mg/kg body weight | 7–10 days | 28 days |

**⚠️ CRITICAL NOTES:**
- Always complete full course — stopping early creates antibiotic resistance
- Do not harvest during withdrawal period
- Record all antibiotic use (legal requirement in most countries)
- Antibiotic sensitivity testing (AST) is highly recommended before use
- Antibiotic resistance is increasing in Vibrio and Aeromonas globally

## BIOSECURITY PROTOCOLS
## ═══════════════════════════════════

### POND PREPARATION BETWEEN CYCLES (Shrimp)

**Step 1: Harvest**
- Drain pond completely
- Remove all remaining animals

**Step 2: Drying (Crucial)**
- Dry pond bottom for 2–4 weeks (until soil cracks)
- Expose to UV sunlight — kills pathogens, breaks disease cycle
- Tilling/plowing pond bottom: 30 cm deep (oxygenate anaerobic layers)

**Step 3: Liming**
- Apply agricultural lime (CaCO₃): 1000–2000 kg/ha
- OR Quicklime (CaO): 500–1000 kg/ha (if disease history)
- Target pH of pond soil: >8.5
- Let sit 1–2 weeks

**Step 4: Water intake**
- Filter inlet water through fine mesh (200–300 micron)
- Chlorinate inlet water: 20–30 ppm chlorine, 24h contact time
- Wait for chlorine to dissipate (or use sodium thiosulfate to neutralize)
- PCR test water for WSSV before stocking

**Step 5: Plankton development**
- Fertilize pond to develop healthy algal bloom (green water)
- Target Secchi disk: 30–40 cm before stocking
- Probiotics application 3–5 days before stocking

**Step 6: PL selection and testing**
- Source from certified SPF hatchery
- PCR test PL for WSSV, EHP, AHPND before stocking
- Acclimatize PLs (temperature + salinity match within ±1 ppt, ±1°C)
- Stock during cool early morning hours

### BIOSECURITY ON-FARM

| Risk Area | Control Measure |
|---|---|
| Bird access | Bird exclusion nets over ponds |
| Wild fish entry | Fine mesh screens on all inlets |
| Crabs/wild shrimp | Perimeter fencing + chlorination |
| Vehicle entry | Disinfection foot bath at farm farm gate |
| Workers | Change clothes + foot dip at pond entry |
| Equipment | Dedicate equipment per pond; disinfect before sharing |
| Dead animals | Remove immediately; bury or dispose; do NOT dump in water body |
| Feed storage | Keep dry, cool, sealed; use within expiry |
| Water exchange | Only from clean source; always screen/filter/chlorinate |

---

### QUARANTINE PROTOCOLS (New Fish/Shrimp)

| Parameter | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Quarantine period | Minimum 14–21 days (fish); 10–15 days (shrimp PLs) |
| Quarantine tank | Separate from main stock; own equipment |
| Health screening | Visual inspection; PCR if warranted |
| Water exchange | 100% every 2–3 days during quarantine |
| Observation | Daily — any sign of disease → test before transfer |
| Treatment | Prophylactic bath treatment if wild-caught |

## EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS
## ═══════════════════════════════════

### EMERGENCY: MASS MORTALITY EVENT

**First 30 minutes:**
1. STOP FEEDING IMMEDIATELY
2. Count and estimate mortality % (use tray/net)
3. Check and record: DO, pH, temperature, water color
4. Collect 10–15 sick/moribund (not dead) animals for diagnosis
5. Do NOT drain pond into natural water bodies

**Within 2 hours:**
1. Run ALL aerators at maximum
2. 20–30% water exchange if water source is clean
3. Remove dead animals (bucket them, do NOT leave in pond)
4. Call aquaculture officer / veterinarian / diagnostic lab
5. Collect samples in ice for laboratory submission

**Sample collection for diagnosis:**
- Wet ice (NOT frozen): 10–15 live/moribund specimens
- Sample should reach lab within 4–6 hours
- Include: pond water sample (500mL), feed sample, records

**Decision matrix:**
- If mortality >20% in 24h → Prepare for emergency harvest
- If WSSV confirmed + pond age >60 days → Immediate harvest
- If WSSV confirmed + pond age <30 days → Difficult; assess economics
- If EMS/AHPND → Evaluate age and weight; harvest if possible

---

### EMERGENCY: OXYGEN CRASH (DO <2 mg/L)

**Immediate response:**
1. Run all aerators/paddlewheels at maximum
2. Stop feeding
3. Splash water on surface (increase air-water contact)
4. Open emergency water exchange
5. Add hydrogen peroxide: 5 mg/L to critical areas
6. Move emergency aeration equipment to worst areas

**DO NOT:**
- Add lime or probiotics during oxygen crash
- Continue feeding
- Create more water disturbance than necessary (stresses fish)

**After stabilization:**
- Find root cause: algae crash? overfeeding? hot weather?
- Increase monitoring: check DO every 2h
- Reduce stocking density if chronic problem

---

### EMERGENCY: pH CRASH (<6.5 for shrimp, <6.0 for fish)

**Immediate:**
1. Stop feeding
2. Apply agricultural lime: 20–50 kg/ha
3. Aerate
4. Emergency water exchange with higher-pH source water

**Causes:**
- Heavy rain (acidic rainwater dilution)
- Peat soil leaching
- Algal bloom collapse overnight
- Low alkalinity

---

### DISEASE OUTBREAK CONTAINMENT

1. Isolate affected pond — no water or equipment sharing
2. Dedicate staff to affected pond (they do NOT go to other ponds)
3. Disinfect exit points: 20 ppm chlorine foot bath
4. Dead animals: bury in lime (1:1 lime:animal) or incinerate
5. NO effluent release to natural waterways
6. Notify local aquaculture authority (mandatory for notifiable diseases)
7. Document everything: date, mortality count, water parameters, actions taken
